The toolkit.
Every element, no libraries.

Each effect below powers mander.ai or austinmander.com. All of it is vanilla canvas + CSS + JS in a single HTML file: no GSAP, no Three.js, no frameworks. View source on any of it.

01 — INK PARTICLE WORDMARK (LIGHT)

Text is drawn to an invisible canvas, its pixels are read back, and every ~3px of opaque ink becomes a particle's home. Particles fly in with per-particle delay, shimmer, and repel from your cursor. The full stop is detected by measuring the bare word's width: everything right of it goes terracotta, at 1.7× size.

02 — MOLTEN PARTICLE WORDMARK (DARK)

Same engine, two changes: additive blending (overlapping particles get brighter, like embers) and a hot palette from deep orange through amber to white-hot. This is the Forgeline variant. Move your cursor through it.

03 — LETTERPRESS SETTLE

Pure CSS keyframes. Each letter is a span with a staggered animation-delay: it arrives blurred, light, and low, then settles sharp and bold, like type being pressed. The dot stamps last with an overshoot curve.

04 — SCROLL-LIT WORDS

Every word is a span at 16% opacity. A scroll listener maps how far the block has travelled through the viewport to a word index; words at or below the index get lit. Key words are flagged to turn terracotta italic. Scroll up and down over this.

Anyone can get AI to a demo now. The distance between a demo and a system a business runs on, that's the work. This sentence lights up as you scroll, one word at a time, and dims when you scroll back.

05 — PINNED NUMERAL FILL

The outer section is 260vh tall; the inner panel is position:sticky so it stays put while you scroll "through" it. Scroll progress drives a clip-path that fills the numeral with terracotta from below, and swaps 01 → 02 → 03. On mobile this collapses to normal flow, because pinning is a desktop pleasure.

06 — VELOCITY MARQUEE

A duplicated strip translated left every frame, wrapping at half its width. Its speed adds your scroll velocity, so it surges when you scroll fast. Flick the page and watch it react.

07 — COUNT-UP STATS

An IntersectionObserver fires when the block enters the viewport; numbers ease to their targets with a quartic curve, so they sprint then land softly. Tabular numerals stop the layout jittering while digits change.

08 — REVEALS + ATMOSPHERE

Everything you've scrolled past used these: elements carry a marker class, an IntersectionObserver adds the revealed state with staggered delays. And the paper texture over this whole page is a single inline SVG turbulence filter, fixed and multiplied at 35% opacity. No image files.

09 — INK FLOW FIELD

Two thousand particles drifting through an invisible vector field made of layered sine waves. Nothing is ever erased: each frame paints a whisper of paper over the last, so the trails bleed like wet ink. The field itself slowly rotates, so the currents never repeat.

10 — HALFTONE PORTRAIT + LOUPE

A photograph, resampled live into print dots: the canvas reads the image's pixels and sizes each ink dot by luminance, like a letterpress halftone plate. Your cursor is a loupe that reveals the real photograph through the dots. The ASCII button re-renders the same luminance map as type instead of dots.

11 — THE FLEET

The brand metaphor, drawn literally: agents orbiting an engineer. Each dot runs its own elliptical orbit with its own speed; every couple of seconds one dispatches to the centre, flashes terracotta, and returns to work. Trails are the same never-erase ink technique as the flow field.

12 — BREATHING TYPE

Fraunces is a variable font with hidden axes: weight, softness, and "wonk". Each letter is a span whose axes oscillate on its own phase, so the word breathes. Bring your cursor close and letters go full wonk, bold and bent, springing back as you leave. No canvas at all, just font-variation-settings at 60fps.

13 — LIQUID INK

Metaballs with no WebGL: soft radial blobs are drawn blurred onto a tiny offscreen canvas, then a contrast filter crushes the blur into a hard edge. Where blobs approach, their fields merge and the ink goes gooey. Your cursor is one of the blobs.

14 — THE PLUCKED RULE

One of the ledger rules from the page background, made physical: a rope of forty points simulated with verlet integration, three constraint passes per frame. Drag your cursor through it and let it twang.
